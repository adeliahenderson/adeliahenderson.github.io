About Me

I will graduate with a Bachelor of Arts in Journalism, including concentrated minors in Criminal Justice and Law & Public Policy, from Mercer University in May 2019.

As a student at Mercer's Center for Collaborative Journalism, I've taken courses related to news writing, community enagagement, photo/video production, coding, investigative reporting and media law/ethics. I've also been published with The Macon Telegraph, GPB Macon, student-run publication EngageCCJ, The Mercer Cluster and the Jones County News.

Currently, I serve as the copy editor for Mercer's university newspaper, The Cluster. During my time at Mercer I have earned a 3.8 grade point average, and was recently awarded the 2018-19 Georgia Press Educational Foundation Morris Newspaper Scholarship.

My Latest Work

Currently I am interning with the United States Department of State, in their Office of Public Engagement.

The Department is launching a new social media campaign, called #State4States, designed to highlight the State Department programs and connections that benefit local communities throughout America. I am using research, writing, graphic design, and social media skills to help the Office of Public Engagement connect diplomacy to a domestic audience.

Every week, the Department features a new state. You can read more about #State4States at their blog, or the Department of State by State map.
